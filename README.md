# T2T-Genome-Assembly-and-Population-Genomic
Population genomic studies in plants require substantial structural variation to uncover environmental adaptation, natural selection, and agronomic traits. T2T reference genomes can be a valuable resource for conserving plant species threatened by climate change. 
In this context, the lack of a gap-free T2T reference genome for K. obovata represents a significant obstacle to advancing population genomics and evolutionary studies in mangrove forests. A complete T2T genome sequence would provide a crucial foundation for accurately characterizing genome-wide variation. The demographic structure of K. obovata populations could shed light on the evolutionary processes shaping genetic diversity in this ecologically important species.
